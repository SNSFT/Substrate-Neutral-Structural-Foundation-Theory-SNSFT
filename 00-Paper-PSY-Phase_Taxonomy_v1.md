## 2. The Fusion Operators

Understanding how the collider produces outputs requires understanding the PSY fusion operators. These are not arbitrary — each one reflects a structural law about how psychological states combine in a real system.

### 2.1 The Five Operators

For an n-body fusion of states with coordinates (P₁, N₁, B₁, A₁), (P₂, N₂, B₂, A₂), ...:

**P — Harmonic Mean (structural compression):**
$$P_{out} = \frac{n}{\sum_{i=1}^{n} \frac{1}{P_i}}$$

The harmonic mean is always ≤ the arithmetic mean. It compresses toward the weakest P value. Two states colliding always produce a P_out lower than either input. More states = more compression. This reflects the structural reality that combining psychological burdens does not increase pattern capacity — it taxes it.

**N — Minimum (narrative bottleneck):**
$$N_{out} = \min(N_1, N_2, ..., N_n)$$

The weakest narrative wins. One state with N < 0.15 collapses the entire output's narrative regardless of how many healthy-N states are also present. This is the N-bottleneck law. It is why shame is so structurally adhesive: a single shame vector (N = 0.05–0.10) collapses the narrative floor of any fusion it enters.

**B — Net Coupling After Cancellation:**
$$k = \sum_{i<j} \min(B_i, B_j) \quad \text{(sum over all C(n,2) pairs)}$$
$$B_{out} = \max(0, \sum_i B_i - 2k)$$

This is the most structurally interesting operator. B values cancel pairwise. When two states have similar B, most of the coupling cancels out — the output has very little behavioral load. When states have very different B values, the residual is the difference. This operator is what generates the DC zone (Section 5.2): two states near the False Lock corridor with B₁ ≈ B₂ cancel almost completely, leaving B_out = |B₁ − B₂| ≈ 0.01–0.02.

**A — Maximum (adaptation ceiling):**
$$A_{out} = \max(A_1, A_2, ..., A_n)$$

The best adaptation capacity in the fusion survives. One IVA-capable state (A > 1.0) preserves IVA capacity in the output regardless of other states' A values. This reflects the structural reality that adaptive capacity is a resource — if any component of the system has it, the whole system can draw on it.

**k-mode (cancellation strategy):**
Two modes were tested:
- **kMode=max:** k = kmax (cancel as much B as possible)
- **kMode=critical:** k < kmax (preserve tension in the output)

This distinction is methodologically critical (Section 4).

### 2.2 Long Division: Two-Beam B-Cancellation

For a 2-beam fusion, the operators reduce to:

$$P_{out} = \frac{2 P_1 P_2}{P_1 + P_2}$$
$$N_{out} = \min(N_1, N_2)$$
$$k = \min(B_1, B_2)$$
$$B_{out} = |B_1 - B_2|$$
$$A_{out} = \max(A_1, A_2)$$

**Step 1 — The equation:** What τ does a 2-beam fusion produce?

$$\tau_{out} = \frac{B_{out}}{P_{out}} = \frac{|B_1 - B_2|}{\frac{2P_1 P_2}{P_1 + P_2}}$$

**Step 3 — Map to PNBA:** The input states are in the B ∈ [0.08, 0.11] band — this is exactly the False Lock and Safety corridor of the PSY corpus. Two states operating near their structural limit, but from different angles, fuse to produce an output with almost no behavioral coupling.

### 6.1 The Finding

### 6.2 The Proof by Fusion Operator

Why is this true? The PSY fusion operators explain it completely.

**Claim:** Any fusion producing τ_out ∈ (0, 0.121) must have N_out ≥ 0.15.

**The N bottleneck:** N_out = min(N₁, ..., Nₙ). For N_out < 0.15, at least one input must have N < 0.15.

**The B-coupling constraint:** States with N < 0.15 in the PSY corpus are shame vectors (SVI: N = 0.05–0.10, B = 0.10–0.14), depression signatures (N = 0.08–0.12, B = 0.03–0.06), and False Lock states (N = 0.07, B = 0.09).

**The key:** For τ_out to land in (0, 0.121), the fusion must produce a low B_out. This requires either near-complete cancellation (max-k mode) or that input B values are small. If an N-collapsed state (B = 0.09–0.14) enters the fusion, one of two things happens:

1. If paired with states of similar B in max-k mode: B cancels nearly completely → τ_out → 0 (Noble), N_out collapses. This is Numbing Noble (N < 0.15, τ = 0). It does not land in the DC zone.

2. If paired with states of very different B in critical mode: B_out is the residual. For τ_out to land below 0.121, B_out must be < 0.121 × P_out ≈ 0.121 × 0.65 ≈ 0.079. But the N-collapsed states that are not near-cancelled will carry their B forward, and the fusion's B_out reflects this. At higher beam counts, the k calculation across all pairs effectively still drives B_out down, but the P compression simultaneously drops P_out, keeping τ elevated.

The net result: N-collapsed states either (a) cancel B completely → Noble with N-collapse, or (b) keep enough B that τ ≥ 0.121. There is no path to τ ∈ (0, 0.121) with N_out < 0.15. The operators block it.

This is not a coincidence. It is the structural reflection of the clinical fact: **a system with collapsed narrative does not operate at low torsion.** Either it goes quiet (Noble-as-numbing), or it stays burdened (IVA / Hidden Load). The middle ground — calm, minimal-load, narrative-intact — requires narrative to be intact. You cannot be genuinely grounded while narratively void.
