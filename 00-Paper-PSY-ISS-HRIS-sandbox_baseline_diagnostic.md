# Applied Identity Physics: The Sandbox Baseline Diagnostic Framework
### Digital Artifact Forensics for High-Resolution Internal Simulations (HRIS) in Persistent Digital Environments

**Applied Identity Physics / HRIS Substrate Framework**

---

## Abstract

Standard clinical paradigms frequently misclassify high-bandwidth cognitive processing in neurodivergent populations as behavioral or executive deficits. When subjected to clinical observation or External Force (F_EXT), internal processing engines shift into protective compression or masking, obscuring their baseline architecture. This paper formalizes the **Sandbox Baseline Diagnostic Framework**, an asynchronous assessment methodology focused on evaluating persistent digital play artifacts — specifically utilizing open-ended sandbox platforms such as *Minecraft*™ and *ARK: Survival Ascended*™ — in zero-External Force (F_EXT) home environments.

No custom task or structured session is required. The artifacts this framework evaluates already exist in any persistent save file accumulated through natural play. By analyzing persistent digital world states, base builds, automation structures, and stat systems through the four core processing vectors in PNBA order — Pattern (P), Narrative (N), Behavior (B), and Adaptation (A) — parents and practitioners can accurately map a child's dominant processing engine without triggering observer bias or environmental friction. This framework extends monotropism theory (Murray, Lesser & Lawson, 2005) by providing a structural taxonomy for *which* attentional axis a child's tunnel runs on, and operationalizes the ecological validity argument established by naturalistic assessment research (Nazneen et al., 2015) in a fully passive, artifact-based form.

## 1. Introduction & Paradigm Shift

To accurately understand how a child processes information, diagnostic tools must bypass verbal communication constraints and observe their unconstrained interaction within persistent digital environments. While analog play provides preliminary cues, open-ended digital sandbox games — most notably *Minecraft*™ and *ARK: Survival Ascended*™ — offer an unprecedented level of persistent structural fidelity.

This model frames observed play behaviors as manifestations of a **High-Resolution Internal Simulation (HRIS)**. Where typical childhood play is impressionistic and transient, HRIS processing operates with strict continuous logic, high geometric/relational fidelity, and maximum internal bandwidth. Digital sandbox saves preserve these operational footprints as static, readable artifacts.

The critical insight of this framework: **a child who has played Minecraft or ARK for any significant period of time has already generated the diagnostic record.** Years of decision history are embedded in every save file — chest organization, abandoned bases, breeding lines, redstone circuits, lore signs. That accumulated record is more honest than any structured clinical task because it was never meant to be evaluated. The audit happens after the fact, asynchronously, against data that accumulated under zero observation pressure.

Research on game-based digital biomarkers has established that natural interactions with commercial off-the-shelf games produce rich, longitudinal behavioral traces that index cognitive and mental health profiles without requiring purpose-built assessment instruments (Torous, Staples & Firth, 2019). The Sandbox Baseline Diagnostic Framework applies this principle specifically to persistent sandbox environments and neurodivergent routing profiles, providing a PNBA-axis taxonomy for reading what those traces encode.

## 2. Prerequisite: The Zero-External Force (F_EXT) Requirement

Before evaluating digital world artifacts, one critical diagnostic condition must be satisfied: **the child must be completely free from External Force (F_EXT).**

In clinical settings, evaluation rooms, or under active real-time monitoring, high-throughput cognitive engines redirect processing power toward defense, masking, or protective compression. An internal simulation engine cannot be accurately observed while the underlying system is actively mitigating environmental torsion (τ) induced by external scrutiny.

This is not a theoretical claim. Hull et al. (2017) demonstrated that autistic individuals employ active camouflaging strategies — masking, compensation, and assimilation — under social observation, redirecting executive bandwidth away from authentic processing toward social defense. Critically, Hull et al. (2021) found that *higher* executive function predicts *greater* camouflaging, meaning the most cognitively intact substrates produce the least visible signal under direct observation. A child who builds obsessively at home but "doesn't know what they like" in a therapist's office is not inconsistent — the substrate is compressed. The clinical session observes the defense layer, not the processing engine.

Consequently, this framework mandates an **asynchronous evaluation methodology**: the child builds unobserved in a safe home environment, and observers audit the persistent digital save file post-hoc. This approach aligns with the ecological validity established by naturalistic assessment research: the Naturalistic Observation Diagnostic Assessment (NODA) demonstrated that clinician-guided in-home video observation achieves 91% diagnostic agreement with gold-standard in-person assessment, while eliminating the observation pressure that distorts clinical baselines (Nazneen et al., 2015). The Sandbox Framework extends this principle further — the artifact accumulates entirely without parent-prompted recording, across months or years of unobserved natural play.

The artifact tells the truth; the clinical session cannot.

## 3. Digital Artifact Forensics: Scope & Exemplars

Direct, live behavioral audits introduce observer bias and trigger external resistance. The Sandbox Framework resolves this by scoping observation to **Digital Artifact Forensics**. Persistent digital worlds preserve complete state histories, automation logic, structural alignments, and environmental modifications — none of which require the child's participation to inspect.

Ringland et al. (2016) demonstrated in an ethnographic study of Autcraft — a Minecraft community for autistic youth — that autistic children spontaneously construct self-regulation tools, spatial organization systems, and relational structures inside Minecraft without being asked or prompted. These emergent artifacts directly encode the child's processing architecture. The Sandbox Baseline Diagnostic Framework provides the structural taxonomy for reading what those artifacts contain.

Two primary games serve as ideal diagnostic exemplars due to their complementary mechanics:

**Minecraft™** — Provides infinite discrete voxel grid alignment, precise redstone logic/automation, and fluid terraforming. Real players accumulate months of structural history across a single world save.

## 4. Theoretical Grounding: Monotropism and PNBA Routing

The Sandbox Baseline Diagnostic Framework is grounded in monotropism theory (Murray, Lesser & Lawson, 2005), which proposes that autistic cognition allocates high-intensity attentional resources to a narrow set of active processing streams — the "attention tunnel" — rather than distributing attention across many simultaneous channels as polytropic (neurotypical) processing does. Monotropism explains the depth of HRIS processing: when a child enters a sandbox environment under zero-F_EXT conditions, their entire attentional bandwidth routes into that single stream, producing the high-fidelity artifact record this framework reads.

Monotropism, however, does not provide a tool for distinguishing *what the tunnel processes*. Two monotropic children both run deep, high-intensity attentional tunnels — but one organizes every item by geometric category (P-axis routing) while the other abandons five half-built bases the moment a superior mechanical solution resolves in simulation (A-axis routing). The behavioral profiles are completely different, the distress triggers are completely different, and the support strategies are completely different. PNBA routing extends monotropism by identifying the *structural direction* of the attentional axis, not just its depth.

Where monotropism theory asks "how deep is the tunnel?", the PNBA routing framework asks "which axis does the tunnel run on?" The artifact provides the answer.

## 5. The Four Baseline Digital Vectors (PNBA Order)

Each section below describes the extreme expression of that routing axis. Most players will recognize their child clearly in one or two of these portraits — that recognition is the diagnostic. No scoring, no structured task, no clinical appointment required.

---

### 5.1 Pattern (P) Dominant: The Architect

**The Action:** Establishing exact spatial alignment, 1:1 scale replicas, rigid chunk symmetry, or strict inventory categorization.

**The Core Drive:** Protecting structural integrity, geometric coherence, and system alignment.

**Digital Artifacts in *Minecraft*:** Perfect symmetrical castle layouts, item sorting chest rooms (sorted strictly by ID/type), 1:1 scale real-world replicas, or exact chunk-aligned perimeter walls.

**Digital Artifacts in *ARK: Survival Ascended*:** Perfectly snapped foundation grids, symmetrical greenhouse layouts, color-coded storage vaults, and flawless snap-point alignment across uneven terrain.

**Standard Play vs. HRIS Processing:**

- *Standard Play:* Placing walls and chests casually for general gameplay functionality; minor structural asymmetries or unsorted storage bins are ignored or resolved casually.
- *HRIS P-Dominant Simulation:* A child constructs a 1:1 symmetrical foundation grid in ARK or a chunk-aligned sorting center in Minecraft. Replacing a single wall piece with an off-center snap point or placing an item in the wrong chest corrupts the entire topological integrity of the build. Distress caused by external disruption is driven by internal phase distortion, not behavioral stubbornness.

---

### 5.2 Narrative (N) Dominant: The Storyteller

**The Action:** Embedding deep historical continuity, persistent role designations, territorial lore, and relational tracking into digital assets.

**The Core Drive:** Maintaining chronological continuity, relational tracking, and contextual lore.

**Digital Artifacts in *Minecraft*:** Inscribed signposts on buildings, custom written books stored in library chests, named mobs/pets with distinct habitats, and territorial boundaries between constructed "factions."

**Digital Artifacts in *ARK: Survival Ascended*:** Custom-named creatures with detailed pedigree folders, tombstone markers for fallen tames, tribe log record-keeping, and bases built around specific roleplay functions (e.g., an "outpost" vs. an "embassy").

**Standard Play vs. HRIS Processing:**

- *Standard Play:* Giving temporary fun names to tames or pets without structural or temporal context.
- *HRIS N-Dominant Simulation:* Managing a persistent digital database across weeks or months. *"Tame #12 cannot be taken on this raid because three weeks ago in the map lore, it was injured at the northern mountain border."* External attempts to override or alter this canon introduce a logically impossible event into a live, high-fidelity relational database.

---

### 5.3 Behavior (B) Dominant: The Kinetic Engine

**The Action:** Stress-testing world boundaries, executing high-speed physical/kinetic loops, and running automated defensive or destructive feedback mechanisms.

**The Core Drive:** Testing active cause-and-effect physics loops through real-time kinetic feedback.

**Digital Artifacts in *Minecraft*:** TNT explosion test craters, complex redstone traps, high-speed minecart rail networks, mob farms, and parkour courses.

**Digital Artifacts in *ARK: Survival Ascended*:** Intricate defense grids with automated turrets, wild creature trapping arenas, ramp/launch setups for land mounts, and high-risk boss arena prep setups.

**Standard Play vs. HRIS Processing:**

- *Standard Play:* Detonating blocks or attacking creatures purely for chaotic visual or sensory stimulation.
- *HRIS B-Dominant Simulation:* Triggering redstone mechanics or automated turret lines from three distinct angles and velocities. The objective is an empirical physics stress test to gather real-time data on pathfinding limits, force vectors, and system resistance.

---

### 5.4 Adaptation (A) Dominant: The Transformer

**The Action:** Continuously pivoting, expanding, or replacing dynamic builds mid-project. Physical completion is secondary to conceptual proof-of-concept.

**The Core Drive:** Exploring state-space possibilities, rapid non-linear iteration, and dynamic variation.

**Digital Artifacts in *Minecraft*:** Maps littered with dozens of half-finished concepts — a partially excavated mountain base, three abandoned redstone door prototypes next to each other, or floating islands built midway before pivoting.

**Digital Artifacts in *ARK: Survival Ascended*:** Multiple starter bases scattered across the map, partially built trap structures abandoned after proof-of-concept, or rapidly shifted creature breeding pens designed for new mutated traits.

**Standard Play vs. HRIS Processing:**

- *Standard Play:* Building a starter base, getting bored after several days, and moving elsewhere on the map.
- *HRIS A-Dominant Simulation:* A child begins building a multi-tier base. Mid-build, the mental simulation resolves a superior ramp/elevator or logic circuit that renders the active structure obsolete. The structure is immediately abandoned or dismantled because the internal simulation solved the mechanical problem instantly. Processing speed moves faster than real-time digital input execution.

## 7. The 3-Step Asynchronous Digital Audit Protocol

**Step 1: Environmental Isolation (Zero-F_EXT Entry)**

Provide access to *Minecraft* or *ARK: Survival Ascended* in a low-stress, unmonitored home environment. Allow uninterrupted session time without soliciting progress reports. If the child already plays either game, this step is already complete — the artifact record exists.

**Step 2: Digital Save Audit (Artifact Inspection)**

When the child is offline, load into the world save or inspect the server state. Evaluate the primary structural footprint following PNBA: grid symmetry (P), lore markers and naming (N), kinetic systems and traps (B), or prototype trails (A). You are not looking for one clean signal — look for what the child returns to, defends most fiercely, or shows the most distress when disrupted. That's the routing axis.

**Step 3: Validation Probe (Gentle Canon Entry)**

Engage the child about their world state using non-disruptive, validating inquiries:

- *For Pattern (P):* "How did you snap these wall foundations so evenly across this hill?"
- *For Narrative (N):* "What is the backstory behind this outpost sign?"
- *For Behavior (B):* "How does this redstone trap / turret grid trigger when a target approaches?"
- *For Adaptation (A):* "What new mechanic made you switch from this old base design to this new one?"

The goal is not to test — it's to signal that the artifact is recognized and valued. That signal alone reduces torsion in future sessions.

## 8. Conclusion

By scoping baseline diagnostics specifically to persistent digital environments like *Minecraft* and *ARK: Survival Ascended*, observers completely bypass F_EXT-induced masking and observation bias. The diagnostic record already exists — accumulated passively across months or years of natural, unobserved play. Auditing digital save states through the P, N, B, and A processing vectors in strict sequence provides a clear, empirical window into a child's High-Resolution Internal Simulation without requiring a single structured clinical session.

The artifact tells the truth. It was built when nobody was watching.
