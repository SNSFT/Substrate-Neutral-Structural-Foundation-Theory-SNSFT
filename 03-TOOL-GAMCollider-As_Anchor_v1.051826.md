2.1 The Arsenic Anchor and Pnictogen Chemistry

Arsenic (As, atomic number 33) serves as our structural anchor. In chemistry,
arsenic is a Group 15 pnictogen — the same family as nitrogen and phosphorus.
Like its cousins, arsenic forms three-coordinate bonds with metals to create
pnictide structures. These structures are the foundation of modern semiconductor
technology: GaAs powers every mobile phone and satellite; InAs enables infrared
imaging. What makes arsenic structurally special is that it bridges three
distinct material families simultaneously:

  - Pnictogen semiconductors: GaAs, InAs, GaP (direct bandgap, optoelectronics)
  - Pnictide superconductors: FeAs-type (LaFeAsO, 2008 Nobel contender)
  - Arsenate/sulfosalt minerals: arsenopyrite (FeAsS), pharmacosiderite

The QuadBeam engine selects for all three families from the As anchor run,
confirming the substrate-neutral nature of the PNBA framework.

2.2 Index of Metamorphic Impact (IM)

  - Academic Definition: IM is a non-dimensional metric quantifying the
    structural reorganization energy of a lattice. It evaluates the net
    thermodynamic drive of a multi-component configuration to shift into
    an altered, deeply stable phase during processing compared to its
    baseline elemental states.

  - Student Analogy: Think of IM as a score for how drastically a recipe
    transforms its raw ingredients. A high IM means the elements don't
    just sit next to each other like a mixed salad; they chemically merge
    into an entirely new, highly unified structure, much like baking raw
    flour, sugar, and eggs into a structural cake.

2.3 Pair-Binding Saturation Coefficient (k)

  - Academic Definition: The k-value represents the cumulative coordination
    index across all six possible elemental pairings within a four-body
    (e1, e2, e3, e4) configuration. It counts the number of interatomic
    links structurally locking the unit cell together.

  - Student Analogy: Imagine atoms as structural nodes using microscopic
    hands to grip each other. The k-value is the total number of hands
    shaking between atoms in the crystal lattice. A higher k-value indicates
    a more interconnected atomic network, which directly translates to a
    denser, more rigid, and tougher material block.

2.4 Residual Bond-Line Stress (B=0)

  - Academic Definition: B measures the latent mechanical strain or
    uncompensated electronic potential remaining in the crystal lattice
    after synthesis. When B=0, the structure has achieved perfect geometric
    and electronic equilibrium.

  - Student Analogy: Imagine building a structure with Lego bricks. If you
    force mismatched pieces together, the plastic bends slightly, leaving
    the model under internal tension; if you drop it, it easily snaps along
    those strained points. A value of B=0 means every single piece snaps
    together perfectly with zero bending. Because there is no built-in stress,
    the final printed component can undergo extreme temperature drops or heavy
    impacts without warping or developing microscopic cracks.

2.5 The Noble Beam Diagnostic (He as Structural Probe)

  - Academic Definition: When an element with B=0 (such as Helium) appears
    in a four-body collision, it contributes zero to all six pairwise coupling
    terms. It is a structural spectator: its presence does not change the
    Noble condition for the other three elements, but it can tip the
    four-body geometry into a Noble ground state that the three-body system
    alone cannot reach.

  - Student Analogy: Think of He as a "fourth chair" at a table for four.
    Three people are trying to find a seating arrangement where everyone
    gets along — but they keep arguing in every combination of two. Adding
    the fourth silent, neutral person changes the group dynamics: everyone
    behaves when the full table is occupied, even though the fourth person
    never says a word. Helium is that fourth person.

  - Why it matters for manufacturing: He-atmosphere synthesis (standard
    industrial practice in titanium and refractory metal processing) is
    structurally validated by this diagnostic. The Noble result is identical
    to vacuum synthesis; the He is purely a carrier.
