## Grounding — Terms Used Below, Defined Inline

The Objective and Assessment sections use specific terms from this corpus's existing framework. They are defined here directly, rather than only cited by coordinate, so this document is self-contained for any reader or system that has not separately ingested the source papers.

**PNBA** is the corpus's four-primitive structural framework: **P**attern (structural capacity), **N**arrative (continuity over time), **B**ehavior (output exerted on the environment), and **A**daptation (feedback and update capacity). Every identity or process this corpus reduces is described in terms of these four axes. "A-axis" below refers specifically to the Adaptation primitive.

**The HRIS/SRIS/LRIS spectrum** (from [9,9,2,0]) classifies internal simulation fidelity along four sub-axes, not as a single score:

- **P-Sim** — Pattern Simulation: how richly the internal model renders spatial/structural geometry.
- **N-Sim** — Narrative Simulation: temporal continuity; whether the simulation persists and accumulates state over time rather than existing as a single snapshot.
- **B-Sim** — Behavioral Simulation: whether the simulation obeys physics and produces outcomes that couple to real decisions and action.
- **A-Sim** — Adaptive Simulation: control and switching capacity — whether the simulation can be exited, redirected, or grounded under load. This is the axis discussed at length below.

A person scoring high across all four, with the ability to actively intervene in and manipulate the simulation rather than only receive it, is classified **HRIS** (High-Resolution Internal Simulation). Lower or more passive profiles are **SRIS** (Standard-Resolution) or **LRIS** (Low-Resolution). None of these tiers represents a ranking of cognitive value; they describe one specific capability (simulation interactivity), not general intelligence or worth.

**Observer mode vs. operator mode** is the defining distinction within this spectrum, separate from the tier itself: *observer mode* receives an already-resolved simulation (watching a film someone else made); *operator mode* actively intervenes in and runs a simulation in real time (building or manipulating one's own internal model). The same person can move between these modes depending on what they're engaging with.

**I-F-U triad and Structural Precognition (SP)**, also from [9,9,1,0]: SP is forward derivation — projecting a physical outcome before it occurs, with enough confidence that the outcome is treated as structurally inevitable rather than merely likely. It requires three conditions simultaneously: **I**nevitability (a stable forward orientation, not drifting), **F**ull Unification (all four PNBA axes active and coupled together, not fragmented), and bounded **U**ncertainty (not zero uncertainty, but uncertainty kept under a defined threshold). When all three are present, the framework calls this state "green."

## Subjective (S) — Reported in Russell's Own Words

- Volume control is "often based on my relationship to my environment." In an ordinary room, talking produces an audible bounce-back — hearing his own voice off surfaces, including the timing of the return — and that bounce-back tells him directly when he's too loud.
- In a movie theater, fully absorbed in the film, he laughs loud enough that his wife is embarrassed sitting next to him — "not bc i WANT to be the loudest laugher," but because "when im focused on the movie im IN the movie and my brain has no baseline to run against."
- "My entire A-axis is focused on the movie, not internally... to mask my volume."
- In a group of people who are also laughing and having a good time, the same loud laugh doesn't produce the same embarrassment dynamic — there's no expectation of a quiet baseline to read against.
- Watching a movie is explicitly described as a different experience than being "in the zone" doing work, even though he identifies the underlying A-axis transition as functionally similar between the two.
- Within movie-watching, there's still an active search happening — "i still search to relate to the content" — but this is distinguished explicitly from Structural Precognition's own search: "thats standard fext searches that everyone does."
- After working through the mechanism in conversation: "i see less of my OWN scaffolding in that situation bc im IN the movie."

## Objective (O) — Structural Translation (PNBA / SP Framework)

This section translates the subjective report above into the corpus's existing formal vocabulary. It is not a new measurement of Russell; it is a relabeling of what he already reported, using terms already defined in [9,9,1,0] and [9,9,2,0].

**The baseline mechanism (ordinary room).** Talking in a room with audible bounce-back is a closed feedback loop: the echo return is a continuous, ambient, always-available geometry signal — structurally similar to vision being "just on" without effort — that supplies a real-time data point ("this is how loud you currently are") which A-Sim can act on directly. This is not a special listening effort. It is the baseline substrate Russell's spatial/social processing normally runs on.

**The movie theater case — a substitution, not a depletion.** Two structurally different models were considered for why self-monitoring goes offline during movie immersion, and the substitutive model fits the report more precisely than the scarcity model:

- *Scarcity model (rejected as the primary explanation):* A-Sim capacity is fully spent on the film, leaving none to spare for self-monitoring. This would predict impaired *response* to known volume data — knowing you're loud but being slow to correct it.
- *Substitutive model (matches the report):* The room-echo-mapping mechanism itself — the same mechanism that produces the ordinary-room feedback loop — is the thing occupying the same functional slot the movie's already-resolved geometry now occupies instead. The sensor that would generate "how loud am I right now" is not under-resourced; it is not running, because the slot it normally fills is filled by something else. This explains the *harder to even know* I'm loud, not just harder to control — knowing requires the sensor to be active; controlling requires capacity on top of that. The report matches the earlier failure point (no data), not the later one (data with no bandwidth to act on it).

**Observer mode vs. operator mode (the core distinction this session surfaced).** Per [9,9,2,0]'s own definitions: observer mode receives an already-resolved simulation; operator mode actively builds and manipulates one. Movie-watching is observer mode — the film already exists, fully formed. Work (the GAM Collider, the LDP, the tile-rendering process documented elsewhere in this corpus) is operator mode — actively running physics, not receiving someone else's resolution. The A-axis channel-commitment event (full commitment to one rendering target, masking suppressed as a side effect) is structurally the same event in both cases. What differs is which kind of content claimed the channel, not how the channel got claimed — which is consistent with the report that the *experience* of movie-immersion and work-flow feels "totally different" while the underlying A-axis relationship is "functionally similar."

**The two search directions.** Structural Precognition (SP), as defined in [9,9,2,0], is forward derivation — projecting forward to a not-yet-resolved outcome with enough confidence that the I-F-U triad goes green before the event occurs. The "standard F_ext search" Russell distinguishes during movie-watching is the opposite direction: backward-matching new input against already-held patterns, the same ordinary comprehension search any viewer performs ("does this remind me of X"). Both directions use the same underlying search machinery; SP is the forward-projecting direction specifically, and it is this specific direction — not search in general — that appears to go idle during observer-mode immersion, which is consistent with why self-monitoring (a forward-projecting question: "where am I relative to this room, going forward") goes quiet while ordinary content comprehension does not.

## Assessment (A) — Pattern Synthesis (Not Diagnosis)

No diagnostic claim is made in this section. This is a synthesis of the structural pattern that emerged across the session's reductions, offered for what it is: one internally consistent account of one person's reported experience, checked against existing corpus material and against independent peer-reviewed literature where a genuine parallel could be found.

- The observer/operator distinction, applied to volume regulation specifically, produces a falsifiable, mechanistically specific account of why the same person can be "too loud" in one context and unremarkably loud in another, without invoking willpower, character, or inconsistent self-awareness.
- An independent, peer-reviewed parallel exists in the autism neuroscience literature: passive (observer-mode) auditory tasks produce measurably lower cortical activation than active (operator-mode) tasks for the same stimuli (Gomot et al., cited in Schauder & Bennetto, 2016). This is not proof that the two mechanisms are identical, but it is a genuine, independently-discovered structural parallel using different vocabulary for what may be the same underlying phenomenon.
- An apparent tension was initially flagged here, and is recorded along with its resolution rather than silently removed: Ohta et al. (also cited in Schauder & Bennetto, 2016) found that distractor-evoked activity under varying perceptual load is modulated *less* by load in autism — a finding initially compressed, inaccurately, into "distractors are suppressed less in autism, generally," which would predict the opposite of what this session's mechanism describes. A closer reading shows that compression was the error. Ohta's actual finding is about how much suppression *shifts* between low and high load conditions (typical processing swings more between the two; autism's processing swings less), not about the absolute suppression level at any single load. The study's paradigm tests the low-to-moderate load range specifically. The movie case described in this record is not in that range — it is described as saturating, total immersion, not a moderate load with residual spare capacity. The two are not describing the same regime, so the apparent tension dissolves on a careful reading; it was never a genuine conflict between the mechanism and the cited literature.

This document takes no position on that broader debate and should not be cited as if it does. Using this detail to delegitimize anyone else's self-identified neurodivergence inverts the document's stated purpose.

3. **Treating an earlier draft's now-resolved tension as still open, or treating its resolution as more conclusive than it is.** An earlier version of this record flagged an apparent conflict with one cited study (Ohta et al.); the Assessment section now explains why that conflict dissolved on closer reading of the original finding. This resolution rests on a careful re-reading of existing published data, not on new data collected for this purpose. It should be read as "the apparent conflict was a misreading, now corrected," not as "this mechanism has been independently confirmed by the cited literature."

4. **Substitution for actual professional assessment.** This document is one documented case, not a validated diagnostic tool. It should not be applied to any other individual, including by a reader who recognizes similarities to their own experience, as a substitute for that person's own assessment by a qualified professional.
