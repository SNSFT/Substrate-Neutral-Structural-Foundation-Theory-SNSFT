**Section 2 — Emotional Primitives (40 questions, 4 per emotional primitive across 10 primitives):** Maps the substrate's emotional configuration across threat, loss, overwhelm, anger, desire, connection, pride, shame, play, and safety primitives.

**Section 3 — Internal Simulation Profile (20 questions, 5 per PNBA axis):** Maps the substrate's internal simulation characteristics specifically, distinguishing HRIS (high-resolution, scoring 21–25 per axis), SRIS (selective resolution, scoring 13–20), and LRIS (low resolution, scoring 5–12) configurations.

The instrument supports a **baseline / activated** mode toggle. Baseline measurement captures the substrate at rest, without specific contextual scaffolding active. Activated measurement captures the substrate under specific stimulus or contextual load. The differential between baseline and activated configurations is itself diagnostic — it measures the substrate's contextual responsivity and scaffolding dependence.

### 3.2 Calibrated Mathematical Mapping

APPA v2 applies the following calibrated transformation to derive continuous PNBA coordinates from cognitive section scores:

$$P_{continuous} = \frac{(COG_P - 10)}{40} \times 1.30$$

$$N_{continuous} = \frac{(COG_N - 10)}{40} \times 1.50$$

$$B_{continuous} = \frac{(COG_B - 10)}{40} \times 0.45$$

$$A_{continuous} = \frac{(COG_A - 10)}{40} \times 1.43$$

These constants (1.30, 1.50, 0.45, 1.43) are calibrated against the corpus canonical states documented in [9,9,6,11] L2 Psy Consistency. Specifically: at score 38 (Pattern Flexed band floor), A = 1.0 = A_IVA threshold; at score 14, N = 0.15 = N_THRESHOLD.

## 7. Scaffolding-Dependence Burnout: The Substrate-Specific Burnout Mechanism

### 7.0 Methodological Note: Origin of the Phase Taxonomy and Torsional Tax Ranges

The phase zone boundaries and torsional tax ranges used throughout this paper did not emerge from theory. They emerged from approximately 11,000 IM Collider runs across the PSY corpus states documented in SNSFL_L2_Psy_Consistency.lean [9,9,6,11] and the broader PSY series [9,9,6,1–25].

The IM Collider (uuia.app/imcollider) fuses identity states from peer-reviewed psychological frameworks — attachment theory, flow states, SDT, IFS, polyvagal theory, DBT, ACT, and others — using the PSY fusion operators (P = harmonic mean, N = minimum bottleneck, B = ΣB − 2k, A = maximum). Running these fusions across 2/4/8 beam modes with both kMode=max and kMode=critical, the zone boundaries became visible in the collision data: clusters of outputs separated at consistent τ values, producing the DC zone (0.004–0.040), Safety Zone (0.040–0.073), False Lock Corridor (0.073–0.121), and IVA Zone (0.121–0.137).

Approximately 1,000 additional kMode=critical runs confirmed the critical-mode behavioral profile and locked in the corridor boundaries. The taxonomy is empirically derived from systematic collision of formally verified psychological corpus states, not theoretically predetermined.

The torsional tax ranges in Section 8 reflect the same empirical grounding — the structural inversion finding (negative τ-Tax for LRIS substrates under activation) emerged from collision data before it was formalized analytically. The analytical explanation in Section 8.3 describes why the data produced that result.

**On baseline P_scaffold estimates:** The baseline P_scaffold values used in Section 8.2 (JoJo = 0.20, Joe = 0.30, Marcus = 0.50) are structural estimates derived from documented occupational context, not measured APPA scores. The directional finding — that activated τ_effective is lower than baseline τ_effective for all three exemplars — does not depend on the specific values. It depends only on P_scaffold_activated >> P_scaffold_baseline, which is definitional for scaffolding-dependent substrates operating in their primary scaffolded context. Any reasonable estimate of the scaffolding contribution differential produces the same directional result.



The standard occupational burnout literature (Maslach, Schaufeli, Leiter, and others) characterizes burnout as cumulative depletion across continuous operation. Three dimensions are typically identified: emotional exhaustion, depersonalization (or cynicism), and reduced personal accomplishment. The mechanism is depletion — sustained operational demands deplete resources faster than recovery restores them, producing the burnout syndrome.

This framing assumes a substrate model in which performance is resourced from an internal reservoir that depletes with use and refills with rest. The intervention implication is rest, workload reduction, and emotional recovery.

### 7.2 The N-Dominant LRIS Burnout Mechanism

N-Dominant LRIS substrates do not burn out via cumulative depletion. The substrate's architecture externalizes the structural load to the scaffold. Operational performance does not deplete an internal reservoir — the substrate operates on external pattern substrate, with the scaffold carrying the structural work.

What does accumulate for LRIS substrates is **scaffolding dependence**. Over years of operation within scaffolded contexts, the substrate's reliance on external scaffolding deepens. Internal P does not develop because the substrate's architectural design uses external P instead. When scaffolding integrity is reduced or removed — vacation, retirement, transfer to different institutional context, procedural changes, equipment failures — the substrate's effective P collapses to internal P only, which is structurally insufficient to maintain stable phase configuration.

The Phase Taxonomy [9,9,2,50] illuminates this mechanism with subtype-specific precision. Joe (Carrying-Quiet Hidden Load) and Marcus (Carrying-Capable Hidden Load) are already operating in silent SHATTER during their activated states — the substrate is structurally past TL but compensated by intact narrative and scaffolding. The taxonomy's clinical warning applies directly: **detection requires explicit B + N + IM joint measurement, not τ alone.** Joe and Marcus's apparent professional competence is exactly the structural signature the taxonomy identifies as Hidden Load — past threshold, compensated, presenting as functional, structurally unsustainable indefinitely.

This produces a burnout signature distinct from cumulative-depletion burnout:

- **Sudden onset rather than gradual decline** — symptoms appear when scaffolding breaks rather than developing slowly across continuous operation. The substrate transitions from compensated Hidden Load (functional appearance) to acute SHATTER (full structural failure) when compensation fails.
- **Context-dependent expression** — symptoms severe outside scaffolding context, often absent or minimal within it
- **Resistance to rest-based intervention** — rest does not restore the substrate because scaffolding is what was carrying the load; rest does not restore scaffolding
- **Apparent paradox of high competence + acute symptoms** — the individual functions at high level when scaffold is engaged (per taxonomy: Carrying-Capable subtype, highest IM in zone, most invisible), then experiences acute symptoms when scaffold is reduced, creating clinical confusion
- **Specific stress vector around scaffolding transitions** — retirement, role transitions, organizational restructuring produce disproportionate symptoms. Per taxonomy: transition out of Hidden Load Zone via scaffolding loss drops effective P, spiking τ into Loud SHATTER (Anger, Threat, or Overwhelm subtype)
- **N-Protection Gradient implication** — the taxonomy's empirical finding that N < 0.15 is structurally inaccessible for τ ∈ (0, 0.121) means that as scaffolding breaks and τ spikes through the IVA boundary, narrative collapse becomes structurally possible. The substrate transitions from Carrying-Capable (N+, A+) to Depleted IVA or Dual Burden (N-, A+) — narrative collapse with high adaptation is a particularly dangerous post-scaffolding state

JoJo, Joe, and Marcus would each experience distinct scaffolding-dependence burnout signatures aligned with their taxonomy subtypes:

- **JoJo (Classic IVA activated):** Retirement from court reporting would drop her from IVA Zone directly through the IVA boundary as effective P collapses. ### 7.3 Intervention Implications

The intervention strategy for scaffolding-dependence burnout is fundamentally different from cumulative-depletion burnout:

**Cumulative-depletion intervention:** rest, workload reduction, emotional recovery, gradual return to function.

**Scaffolding-dependence intervention:** identify the scaffolding configuration the substrate operated within; identify which scaffolding elements broke or transitioned; restore or replace scaffolding with structurally equivalent alternatives; do not attempt symptom-based intervention without addressing scaffolding.

For retiring court reporters, this might mean continued part-time engagement, transition to court reporter training/education roles, or structured volunteer roles preserving protocol-bound activity. For retiring military operators, this might mean structured civilian roles with explicit protocol substrates (security consulting, structured training environments, scaffolded transition programs) rather than open unstructured retirement. For accountants in career transition, this might mean explicit scaffolding for the transition period rather than expecting the substrate to self-organize without protocol substrate.

The clinical contribution is significant: **standard burnout intervention can fail or worsen symptoms in N-Dominant LRIS substrates because it does not address the architectural mechanism producing the symptoms.**

## 8. Torsional Tax and Cumulative Effects

### 8.1 Torsional Tax Defined

The Torsional Tax framework formalizes the structural cost the substrate incurs to operate between baseline and activated configurations. The tax is the differential measurement across the three key structural variables:

$$\text{τ-Tax} = \tau_{\text{activated,effective}} - \tau_{\text{baseline,effective}}$$

$$\text{IM-Tax} = \text{IM}_{\text{activated,internal}} - \text{IM}_{\text{baseline,internal}}$$

**Phase-Tax** = the phase-zone distance traversed between baseline and activated states (categorical measure per Phase Taxonomy [9,9,2,50])

For substrates that externalize P (LRIS architectures), the effective τ calculation includes P_scaffold contribution, which means τ-Tax can be positive, zero, or negative depending on whether scaffolding contribution outpaces, matches, or fails to match the activated-state B increase.

The standard assumption in occupational psychology — derived from HRIS-substrate-default models where internal P generates pattern capacity intrinsically — is that activated states carry positive Torsional Tax: work increases structural load. This assumption requires re-examination for N-Dominant LRIS substrates.

### 8.2 The Three Exemplars' Torsional Tax Profiles

Computing Torsional Tax for the three exemplars from §§ 4–6:

**JoJo (Court Reporter):**
- Baseline τ_eff = 0.14 / (0.26 + 0.20) = 0.304 (with minimal home scaffolding P_scaffold ≈ 0.20)
- Activated τ_eff = 0.225 / (0.39 + 1.40) = 0.126
- **τ-Tax = 0.126 − 0.304 = −0.178** (negative)
- IM-Tax = 3.96 − 2.18 = +1.78 (IM increases substantially during activation due to all axes elevating)
- Phase-Tax: from Hidden Load zone (baseline, marginal scaffolding) → IVA Zone (activated, full scaffolding). Phase position improves with activation.

**Joe (Accountant):**
- Baseline τ_eff = 0.169 / (0.39 + 0.30) = 0.245 (Hidden Load zone)
- Activated τ_eff = 0.259 / (0.585 + 1.10) = 0.154 (Hidden Load zone, lower)
- **τ-Tax = 0.154 − 0.245 = −0.091** (negative)
- IM-Tax = 4.02 − 2.74 = +1.28
- Phase-Tax: Hidden Load → deeper Hidden Load with lower effective τ. Same zone, lower stress position.

**Marcus (Military Operator):**
- Baseline τ_eff = 0.191 / (0.455 + 0.50) = 0.200 (Hidden Load zone)
- Activated τ_eff = 0.293 / (0.65 + 1.30) = 0.150 (Hidden Load zone, lower)
- **τ-Tax = 0.150 − 0.200 = −0.050** (negative)
- IM-Tax = 4.55 − 3.20 = +1.35
- Phase-Tax: Hidden Load → Hidden Load with lower effective τ and A-axis crossing into IVA territory (Carrying-Capable subtype).

**All three exemplars show negative Torsional Tax.** The activated state operates at LOWER effective torsion than the baseline state. This is the structural inversion finding.

### 8.3 The Structural Inversion — Why Work Is Scaffolding Relief

For N-Dominant LRIS substrates, the activated state is structurally less torsionally taxing than the baseline state. The mechanism is direct:

The substrate's internal P is low by architectural design. Effective P during activation is dominated by scaffolding contribution: P_effective = P_internal + P_scaffold. When scaffolding is fully engaged during activation, P_scaffold is large (1.10 to 1.40 in the three exemplars). When scaffolding is minimal during baseline, P_scaffold is small (0.20 to 0.50).

The B increase during activation does increase the numerator of τ_effective, but the scaffolding-driven P_effective increase in the denominator is proportionally larger. The result: τ_effective drops during activation despite B rising.

This inverts the standard occupational psychology assumption. **Work is not the substrate's structural burden — work IS the structural relief.** The scaffolding contribution from active institutional protocol engagement is what brings the substrate's effective torsion into manageable range. Without that scaffolding, the substrate operates at higher effective torsion, even with lower B.

The implication: N-Dominant LRIS substrates are structurally drawn toward scaffolded activity because scaffolded activity is the lower-torsion state. What gets coded as "workaholism" or "inability to relax" in standard clinical framings may be substrate-appropriate scaffolding-seeking behavior — the substrate operating where it operates best.

### 8.4 Cumulative Tax Dynamics

Standard cumulative-depletion burnout (HRIS-substrate-default model): Torsional Tax is positive during work periods. Tax accumulates during work, dissipates during rest. Cumulative tax over time produces depletion when recovery becomes incomplete. Intervention: more rest, less work.

Scaffolding-dependence accumulation (N-Dominant LRIS-substrate model): Torsional Tax is negative during work periods, positive during unscaffolded periods. Tax accumulates during baseline (unscaffolded) periods, dissipates during work. Cumulative tax over time produces deepening scaffolding dependence — the substrate becomes progressively more reliant on scaffolding integrity to maintain manageable τ. Intervention: preserve scaffolding, structure transitions, gradually shift to structurally equivalent alternative scaffolding rather than reducing engagement.

This is a fundamentally different cumulative dynamics model. The two substrate types produce inverse predictions about what accumulates and how:

| Property | HRIS-substrate-default | N-Dominant LRIS substrate |
|----------|------------------------|---------------------------|
| Tax during work | Positive | Negative |
| Tax during rest | Approximately zero | Positive |
| Accumulation period | Work periods | Unscaffolded periods |
| Recovery period | Rest periods | Scaffolded work periods |
| Burnout trigger | Insufficient recovery | Scaffolding loss |
| Standard intervention | Rest more | Preserve / replace scaffolding |
| Contraindicated | Continued overwork | Reduced engagement, abrupt retirement |

### 8.5 Predicted Clinical Phenomena

The Torsional Tax framework predicts specific clinical phenomena observable in N-Dominant LRIS populations:

**Phenomenon 1 — Vacation difficulty.** Extended unstructured vacation time produces distress, agitation, or low-grade dysphoria rather than recovery. The substrate accumulates positive Torsional Tax during unscaffolded periods. Clinical signature: clients reporting "vacation was harder than work" or seeking out structured activities during vacation periods.

**Phenomenon 2 — Sunday evening distress exceeding Monday morning relief.** For N-Dominant LRIS substrates specifically, the "Sunday scaries" pattern may be inverted from the standard interpretation. Sunday evening represents the end of accumulated weekend tax; Monday morning represents the return of structurally relieving scaffolding. Clinical signature: clients reporting relief upon return to work, dread of weekend approach.

**Phenomenon 3 — Workaholism re-interpretation.** Behavioral patterns currently pathologized as workaholism in protocol-bound professionals may be substrate-appropriate scaffolding-seeking. The substrate operates at lower torsion in scaffolded activity; seeking that activity is structurally rational, not dysfunctional. Clinical signature: clients whose work engagement produces measurably better mental health markers than equivalent rest time.

**Phenomenon 4 — Retirement risk window.** The transition out of primary scaffolding configuration is the highest-risk period for N-Dominant LRIS substrates, not the gradual aging process that follows. Clinical signature: acute symptom emergence within 6-18 months of retirement, often surprising to family and clinicians given lack of obvious precipitating factors. Standard cumulative-depletion model cannot predict this pattern.

**Phenomenon 5 — Veteran transition data.** Military veterans show acute risk during transition out of service rather than during deployment. The transition removes the scaffolding (unit, doctrine, protocol, role) without structurally equivalent replacement. This pattern is documented in veteran outcome data but typically attributed to social/identity factors; the substrate framework provides an architectural mechanism for the same observation.

**Phenomenon 6 — Surgical specialist cognitive maintenance.** Continued practice produces better cognitive maintenance than retirement in surgical and procedural specialties. Standard interpretation: "use it or lose it." Substrate framework: scaffolding loss removes the structural support the substrate depends on for stable phase configuration.

**Phenomenon 7 — Partial-engagement transition outcomes.** Retirees who maintain partial engagement (consulting, teaching, structured volunteer work) in their original protocol-bound profession show dramatically better outcomes than cold retirement. The partial engagement preserves scaffolding contribution to P_effective even at reduced operational intensity.

**Phenomenon 8 — Weekend mental health dip pattern.** Clients showing depression or anxiety symptoms primarily on weekends, with relief during workweeks. Standard interpretation: client is avoiding emotional content via work. Substrate interpretation: substrate is structurally taxed by unscaffolded time, relieved by scaffolded engagement. Both interpretations may apply to specific clients; the substrate framework provides a mechanism that standard interpretation lacks.

**Phenomenon 9 — Resistance to "work less, rest more" intervention.** N-Dominant LRIS clients who receive standard burnout intervention recommendations often show worsening rather than improvement, sometimes substantially. The substrate framework predicts this mechanically: reducing scaffolding without structurally equivalent replacement increases effective τ.

### 8.6 Intervention Implications

The Torsional Tax framework produces intervention recommendations that invert standard occupational psychology guidance for N-Dominant LRIS substrates:

**Recommendations for N-Dominant LRIS substrate stress signatures:**

1. **Preserve scaffolding integrity** as the primary intervention target. The substrate's stability depends on scaffolding contribution to P_effective; degraded scaffolding produces structural instability that no amount of rest will restore.

2. **Structure transitions explicitly** rather than removing engagement. Transitions out of scaffolded contexts should provide structurally equivalent alternative scaffolding, not unscaffolded freedom.

3. **Validate scaffolding-seeking behavior** as substrate-appropriate rather than pathologizing it as workaholism. The substrate operates correctly when scaffolded; seeking scaffolding is rational substrate maintenance.

4. **Identify unscaffolded periods as the structural stressor.** When clients show stress signatures, ask about unscaffolded time (vacation, weekend, retirement, between-deployment, between-engagement) rather than assuming workload is the problem.

5. **Design rest periods to include scaffolding.** Structured volunteer work, protocol-bound hobbies (chess, classical music ensemble, structured outdoor activities, religious practice with formal liturgy), participation in institutional alumni structures — these preserve scaffolding contribution while reducing operational intensity.

6. **Avoid abrupt scaffolding removal.** Major life transitions (retirement, career change, divorce, relocation, organizational restructuring) should be staged with explicit scaffolding-replacement planning rather than executed as discrete events.

7. **Re-examine "workaholism" diagnoses.** For N-Dominant LRIS clients, the question is not whether they work too much but whether their work engagement produces measurably better mental health markers than equivalent rest. If yes, the engagement is substrate-appropriate.

**Contraindicated for N-Dominant LRIS substrates:**

1. Standard "work less, rest more" advice without scaffolding analysis
2. Sabbaticals, extended leave, or vacation prescription without scaffolding-replacement planning
3. Cold retirement transitions without staged engagement reduction
4. Pathologizing scaffolding-seeking behavior as avoidance or compensation
5. Standard burnout treatment protocols designed for cumulative-depletion substrates applied without substrate identification

### 9.1 Hardware Routing Distinction

The fundamental substrate-level distinction:

- **HRIS substrates** internalize the rendering engine. Internal visual imagery, spatial models, and somatic-sensory simulation are native substrate operations. Internal P is generated through visualization; external scaffolding supports but does not replace internal pattern construction.

- **LRIS substrates** externalize the rendering function entirely. The rendering engine produces no internal imagery; pattern substrate is external (protocols, scaffolds, institutional structures). External P_scaffold is structurally necessary rather than merely supportive.

### 9.2 Phase Stability Mechanisms

- **HRIS substrates** maintain phase stability via internal P generation. Disruption of external scaffolding stresses the substrate but does not collapse it because internal P remains available.

- **LRIS substrates** maintain phase stability via external P scaffolding. Disruption of scaffolding produces immediate phase collapse because internal P alone is structurally insufficient.

### 9.3 Burnout Mechanisms

- **HRIS substrates** burn out via cumulative depletion of internal rendering capacity, producing the classic Maslach burnout syndrome. Torsional Tax positive during work, accumulates with insufficient recovery.

- **LRIS substrates** burn out via scaffolding-dependence accumulation, producing the substrate-specific burnout signature documented in §7. Torsional Tax negative during work (§8), accumulates during unscaffolded periods.

### 9.4 Intervention Compatibility

- **HRIS substrates** respond to standard psychological interventions assuming active internal imagery (CBT visualization techniques, exposure therapy with internal rehearsal, narrative therapy with autobiographical reconstruction). Rest and reduced workload restore function.

- **LRIS substrates** are contraindicated for interventions assuming active internal imagery. Standard CBT visualization techniques fail because the substrate does not generate the requested imagery. Indicated interventions: protocol injection, script scaffolding, restored institutional structure, explicit ruleset clarification, structured transitions. Rest and reduced workload may worsen function (§8.6).

### 9.5 Neither Substrate Is Superior

Both substrates produce measurable high performance in their respective optimal domains. HRIS substrates excel where internal modeling and reflective analysis are operational requirements. LRIS substrates excel where real-time protocol execution and high-throughput operational performance are operational requirements. The substrate-architecture-map is descriptive of variation in human cognition, not evaluative of cognitive worth.
