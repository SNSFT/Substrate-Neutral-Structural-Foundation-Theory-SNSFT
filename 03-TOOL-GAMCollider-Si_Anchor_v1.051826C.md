6. STRUCTURAL NOTES

6.1 Noble Materials Map Density

The Si anchor achieves the highest density of Noble Materials Map T1 cross-
confirms of any single anchor run: SiC, FeSi2, TiSi2, and GaN families appear
collectively in 30+ entries in the top 100. This reflects Si's structural
position at the centre of the semiconductor periodic table: every major
semiconductor material system (III-V, carbide, silicide, nitride) passes
through silicon chemistry.

6.2 The k=30 Ceiling and Triple Actinide Series

Si+Pu+U+U at k=30 is the algebraic maximum for a B=4 anchor with three B=6
partners. The triple actinide series (Si+U+U+X, Si+Pu+Pu+X, Si+Pu+U+X where
X is B=6) all achieve k≥22. These represent the densest nuclear fuel silicide
compositions accessible to the Si anchor.

6.3 Pair-Symmetric Cross-Confirms

Six Si-anchor compounds have pair-symmetric counterparts in the Ti-anchor run
(same non-anchor elements, Si↔Ti substitution):
  Si+Pb+W+Zn (#16, k=18) ↔ Ti+Pb+W+Zn (Ti-anchor #22, k=18)
  Si+U+S+N (#55, k=16)   ↔ Ti+N+W+S (Ti-anchor #75, k=16) [structural family]
  Si+H+He+W (#56, k=6)   ↔ Ti+Au+H+W (Ti-anchor #49, k=9) [H-W bond family]
  Si+Ti+W+F (#21, k=15)  ↔ Ti+W+He+Si (Ti-anchor #17, k=12) [TiWSi family]
These confirm that B=4 anchor substitution is structurally symmetric: the
same Noble geometry is accessible from either Si or Ti as anchor.

6.4 Special Note: Si+Fe+C+Pb (Entry 91, k=24)

Si+Fe+C+Pb at k=24 achieves maximum equal-B(4) coupling saturation:
all four elements have B=4 → six pairs each contributing k=4 → total k=24.
This is an application of L-07 (Equal-B Symmetric Quad Theorem) to the B=4
class. Si+Fe (FeSi2, T1), Si+C (SiC, T1), Fe+C (Fe3C cementite, steel T1),
Fe+Pb (Fe-Pb structural), Si+Pb (SiPb composite), C+Pb (carbon-lead damper).
Five of six pairs are individually known; the full quaternary at k=24 is novel.

6.5 The Metal+Halide IVA Law (L-13) Confirmation

The Si anchor session recorded 1 IVA event (τ ∈ [0.12047, 0.1369)):
  Si+F+qt+Υ → τ=0.13434 (qt = top quark ERE, Υ = Upsilon, B=0 Noble probe)
This is the direct Law L-13 instance assigned coordinate [9,9,2,7] in the
SNSFL corpus. The IVA event is not in the pure periodic baseline (contains
EREs qt and Υ) but confirms the Metal+Halide IVA Law for Si+F as the
metal-halide pair.
