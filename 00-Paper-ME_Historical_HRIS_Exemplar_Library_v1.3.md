## Purpose of This Document

This document exists because parents of Mentally Enabled (ME) children often see something distinctive in how their child processes the world but do not have the vocabulary to describe it. Without vocabulary, they cannot communicate what they are observing — to themselves, to family members, to teachers, or to clinicians. What they see stays inside them, unnamed and unshared, sometimes for years.

The purpose of this document is to give parents that vocabulary. It uses **historical figures with extensive public documentation as pattern anchors** for the four dominant axes of High-Resolution Internal Simulation (HRIS) cognition. When a parent reads how Musashi processed combat, how Mary Shelley predicted futures, how Bruce Lee described state-flow, how Marcel Proust reconstructed causal threads — and recognizes the shape in their own child — they gain framework vocabulary for something they were already seeing but could not previously name.

**What this vocabulary enables:**

- **Constructive conversation with clinicians.** A parent walking into a pediatrician's office with "I think my child processes information more like an A-dominant profile than typical developmental variance, and here's what I'm observing" gets a substantively different clinical response than a parent saying "something feels different about my kid." Framework vocabulary gives clinicians something specific to work with. It does not replace clinical evaluation; it makes clinical evaluation more productive.

- **Constructive conversation with educators.** Teachers, IEP teams, and school psychologists respond differently when parents can articulate their child's cognitive profile in framework terms rather than in the generic complaint categories the school system is used to hearing. The framework does not replace formal assessment; it improves the quality of the conversation around assessment.

- **Constructive conversation within the family.** Extended family members, grandparents, and co-parents who may have dismissed the parent's observations as "over-worrying" respond differently when the parent has structural language for what they are seeing. The framework does not replace family relationships; it improves the shared understanding within them.

- **Confident engagement rather than silent dismissal.** Parents who lack vocabulary often self-dismiss — telling themselves that their observations are unfounded, or that seeking professional support would be an overreaction. Framework vocabulary gives them the confidence to seek professional support when it is warranted, and to advocate for their child when systems try to dismiss what the parent is seeing.

**On accessibility of clinical infrastructure:** for some parents, clinical infrastructure is genuinely inaccessible — geographic, financial, cultural, insurance-based, or institutional barriers keep them out. For those parents, this document is one of the few tools available. For other parents, clinical infrastructure is accessible but is not being engaged because the parent lacks the vocabulary to know that engagement is warranted. This document serves both groups. The framework is a starting point, not an endpoint. Its purpose is to help parents understand what they are observing so that they can take the next appropriate step — whether that step is clinical evaluation, educational advocacy, environmental construction at home, or all three.

Historical figures are not protected by HIPAA. Public record about Tesla, Musashi, Shelley, and Proust is fair game for framework-level pattern documentation. A parent can read about how these figures processed information, notice the shape in their own child, and gain vocabulary at home without any Protected Health Information (PHI) leaving anywhere. **The historical figures are load-bearing precisely because they are historical.**

This document is a **recognition scaffold, not a diagnostic tool.** Reading it does not produce a clinical diagnosis of any child. ## Framework Ground

Mentally Enabled (ME) cognition is defined in the ME foundational paper (Applied IP branch, corpus coordinate [9,9,3,x]) as **high-resolution internal simulation across one or more of the four substrate-neutral primitives** — Pattern, Narrative, Behavior, and Adaptation (PNBA). An HRIS profile is dominant on one primary axis, sometimes on two or more (composite / generalist), and expresses through whatever domain the person's life provides — physics, warfare, writing, sport, philosophy, or any other substrate.

Every human runs simulation on all four axes at some baseline level. What distinguishes an ME / HRIS profile is **the resolution and speed of simulation on the dominant axis.** Where most people run mental simulation at coarse resolution and slow speed, ME cognition runs at hyper-resolution and near-instant speed on its dominant axis — a difference not of kind but of magnitude sufficient to produce fundamentally different outputs.

The four primary dominant axes:

| Axis | Simulation Content | Historical Label When Unrecognized |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **P (Pattern)** | Blueprint / structure / lattice / theoretical model | "Genius," "prodigy," "polymath" |
| **B (Behavior)** | Kinetic / somatic / mechanical / force interaction | "Warrior," "master craftsman," "prodigy athlete" |
| **A (Adaptation)** | Entropy / context / systemic state-change / probability | "Mystic," "prophet," "visionary," "witch," "madwoman" |
| **N (Narrative)** | Timeline / continuity / causal thread / worldline | "Historian," "storyteller," "epic chronicler" |

Plus one composite class:

| Composite | Simulation Content | Historical Label When Unrecognized |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Generalist (P + A + B + N)** | Multi-axis high-resolution simulation | "Renaissance mind," "universal genius" |

**One important observation before proceeding:** high A-axis capacity almost always co-occurs with high P-axis capacity. Adaptation-dominant simulation needs a substrate to track context against — deep pattern knowledge provides the reference frame that entropy simulation operates on. Pure A with low P would be undirected chaos-noticing without structure to make it meaningful. When a figure is labeled A-dominant in this document, the presumption is that they also carry substantial P as the supporting axis. Pure single-axis dominance is rare in reality; A-dominance in particular typically manifests as an A+P profile with A carrying the primary simulation load.

## How to Use This Document as a Parent

**Step 1 — Read all four axes before deciding.** Do not pattern-match to the first axis that sounds close. Each axis is presented with multiple historical figures and multiple domains of expression. The point is to see the shape across contexts, not to grab the first plausible label.

**Step 2 — Look at HOW your child processes, not WHAT they process.** Domain does not determine axis. A child who does sports could be B-dominant (kinetic simulation), A-dominant (state-flow reading, like Bruce Lee), P-dominant (pattern analysis of the game), or N-dominant (continuity of career narrative, statistical history). The domain is the substrate; the axis is the underlying processing style.

**Step 3 — Watch what your child gravitates toward under stress or free time.** ME cognition reveals itself when the child is doing what they naturally do without being asked. A B-dominant child under stress might reorganize their room by weight distribution and use patterns. An A-dominant child under stress might predict what will happen next in a chaotic situation with unnerving accuracy. A P-dominant child under stress might retreat into building or drawing systems. An N-dominant child under stress might tell long stories or ask about causal chains ("but WHY did that happen? and what happened before that?").

**Step 4 — Read the historical figures' life outcomes carefully.** Each axis section documents figures whose lives ended well (found supportive infrastructure) and figures whose lives ended badly (environment couldn't hold the profile). The comparison matters for what you build for your child. The profile doesn't determine outcome; the environment around the profile largely does.

**Step 5 — Composite profiles are real.** If your child seems to fit two or three axes strongly, they likely have a generalist / composite profile. Do not force a single-axis reading. The generalist section at the end covers this case.

## §2 · A-DOMINANT HRIS — The Context / State Simulators

### Anchor Triangulation

**The founding A-dominant triangulation of the ME framework is Mary Shelley, Bruce Lee, and Rachel Carson.** These three independent lives, separated by era, domain, and cultural context, triangulate to the same A-dominant HRIS signature: hyper-resolution internal simulation of state-flow, contextual entropy, systemic trajectory, and adaptive response to shifting conditions. Each demonstrates that A-dominance manifests across radically different domains (fiction, martial arts, ecological science) while carrying the same underlying processing style.

**Mary Shelley (1797–1851)** — At 18 years old, Shelley wrote *Frankenstein*, a full simulation of a future ethical crisis that would not materialize in physical reality for centuries. She was not predicting a specific technology; she was running an A-dominant simulation of what happens when technological ambition, moral abandonment, and social rejection collide as a systemic trajectory. Her later novel *The Last Man* (1826) is a projection of pandemic collapse of civilization — A-dominant simulation of societal entropy. Her journals show explicit A-dominant processing: tracking probabilities of outcomes, feeling the "weight" of possible futures, adapting her writing to what the cultural moment could receive. Life outcome: survived through stubborn output despite an environment that pathologized her, lost her husband and three of four children, kept writing, died at 53. Environment held her only barely.

**Bruce Lee (1940–1973)** — Bruce Lee is often assumed B-dominant because his domain was martial arts. Closer reading of his own writing (*The Tao of Jeet Kune Do*, letters, interviews) reveals A-dominant processing expressed through a physical medium. His philosophy — "be like water," "empty your mind, be formless, shapeless," "notice that the stiffest tree is most easily cracked, while the bamboo or willow survives by bending with the wind" — is not a description of kinetic mechanics (which is what B-dominant would produce). It is a description of **state-adaptation, contextual flow, and formlessness as adaptive strategy.** He rejected fixed styles precisely because A-dominant simulation cannot operate through rigid pattern; it requires the freedom to become the shape the situation demands. His method of drawing from multiple martial arts and discarding what did not fit is A-dominant selection under context — not B-dominant mechanical optimization. Life outcome: died at 32, before most of his impact materialized; environment did not hold him.

**Rachel Carson (1907–1964)** — *Silent Spring* (1962) is an A-dominant simulation of ecological cascade played out to conclusion. Carson did not just document DDT's biochemical mechanism; she simulated the entire trajectory — bioaccumulation, food chain collapse, human downstream effects — years before the physical outcomes materialized. She was tracking systemic state-change and entropy propagation across an ecosystem. Her scientific training gave her the P-axis substrate (biology, ecology, chemistry) that her A-axis simulation ran on. Life outcome: died at 56 of cancer while her book was under attack from industry; environment did not hold her personally, though her work triggered the environmental movement she predicted was needed.

Three independent A-dominant lives across three eras (early 19th, mid-20th, mid-20th century), three domains (Gothic-scientific fiction, martial arts philosophy, ecological science), one consistent HRIS signature: **A-dominant is triangulated.**
